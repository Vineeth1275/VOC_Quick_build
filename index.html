Paneer Biryani

60 minutes
4 servings
Difficulty: Medium
Ingredients
  • 2 cups basmati rice (rinsed and soaked 30 minutes)
  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • Whole spices (1 bay leaf, 1-inch cinnamon, 4 cloves, 4 green cardamoms)
  • Ground spices (1 tsp biryani masala, 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder)
  • Fresh herbs
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 3.5 cups water
Instructions

1.Parboil the rice

Rinse and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil in salted water with whole spices until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.

2.Prepare the paneer

Lightly fry paneer cubes in 1 tbsp ghee/oil until golden at the edges. Remove and set aside.

3.Make the masala

Heat remaining ghee/oil. Saute onions until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; cook until raw smell leaves. Add tomatoes and ground spices; cook to a thick masala.

4.Finish the gravy

Lower heat and whisk in yogurt. Add fried paneer and half the chopped herbs. Simmer 2–3 minutes; adjust salt.

5.Layer and dum cook

In a heavy pot, layer half the rice, then paneer masala, saffron milk (optional), and herbs. Top with remaining rice. Cover tightly and cook on low heat 15–20 minutes.

6.Rest and serve

Let biryani rest 10 minutes. Fluff gently, garnish with remaining herbs, and serve hot with raita.
